XSCALE: XP for the Enterprise

by Peter Merel - Apr 21st, 2014 - posted in Autonomous, Continuous, Ecosystem, Lean, Scalable, XP

XSCALE is a pattern language for a continously delivering, continously self-organizing, continuously adaptive Agile enterprise that turns all the dials to ten.

Why XSCALE?

Scrum is basically just watered-down XP. It’s more permissive than XP, which was good because that made it easier for teams to do and enabled it to be used in more contexts than just product development. But it was also bad because it was too permissive of bad practices in product development, which is why so few development teams do “just Scrum”, the majority adopting at least part of XP whether they know it or not.
